Water quality data in this dashboard comes from the Scottish Environment Protection Agency (SEPA) and reflects classification under the Water Framework Directive (WFD) — a piece of EU-derived legislation that Scotland continues to apply through the Water Environment and Water Services (Scotland) Act 2003.

The overall status is determined by whichever element — biological, chemical, or hydromorphological — scores worst. Scotland's target is for all water bodies to achieve at least Good status by 2027.

Pressures on water quality

Common pressures affecting water quality in Scotland include agriculture (diffuse pollution from nutrients and sediment), urban drainage, historic land use, and physical modification of watercourses such as straightening or culverting. SEPA publishes River Basin Management Plans setting out how these pressures will be addressed.

For detailed classification data and the latest River Basin Management Plan, see SEPA's water pages.
